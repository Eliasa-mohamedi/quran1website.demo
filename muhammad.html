The Life of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

From Birth to Legacy

▼
🎨

وَمَا أَرْسَلْنَاكَ إِلَّا رَحْمَةً لِّلْعَالَمِينَ

"And We have not sent you, [O Muhammad], except as a mercy to the worlds."

— Surah Al-Anbiya (21:107)

🌟
570 CE

Birth in Makkah

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was born in Makkah in the Year of the Elephant (570 CE). His father Abdullah had passed away before his birth, and his mother Aminah named him Muhammad, meaning "the praised one." He was born into the noble Quraysh tribe, from the clan of Banu Hashim.

أَنَا دَعْوَةُ أَبِي إِبْرَاهِيمَ

"I am the supplication of my father Ibrahim." — Hadith (Musnad Ahmad)

👶
570-576 CE

Early Childhood

Following Arab custom, the infant Muhammad ﷺ was sent to the desert to be nursed by Halimah al-Sa'diyah. During this time, the miraculous opening of his chest occurred. His mother Aminah passed away when he was six, and his grandfather Abdul Muttalib took guardianship, followed by his uncle Abu Talib.

أَلَمْ نَشْرَحْ لَكَ صَدْرَكَ

"Did We not expand for you your breast?" — Surah Ash-Sharh (94:1)

⭐
576-595 CE

Youth: Al-Amin (The Trustworthy)

As a young man, Muhammad ﷺ became known as "Al-Amin" (the Trustworthy) and "As-Sadiq" (the Truthful) for his impeccable character. He worked as a shepherd and later as a merchant. He participated in the Hilf al-Fudul (League of the Virtuous), a pact to protect the oppressed.

وَإِنَّكَ لَعَلَىٰ خُلُقٍ عَظِيمٍ

"And indeed, you are of a great moral character." — Surah Al-Qalam (68:4)

💝
595 CE

Marriage to Khadijah (RA)

At age 25, Muhammad ﷺ married Khadijah bint Khuwaylid (RA), a respected businesswoman of Makkah. She was his first wife and greatest supporter. Their marriage was blessed with children including Fatimah (RA). Khadijah (RA) was the first to accept Islam.

خَيْرُ نِسَائِهَا مَرْيَمُ وَخَيْرُ نِسَائِهَا خَدِيجَةُ

"The best of its women is Maryam and the best of its women is Khadijah." — Sahih Bukhari

📖
610 CE

First Revelation in Cave Hira

At age 40, while meditating in Cave Hira during Ramadan, Angel Jibreel (AS) appeared with the first revelation. This marked the beginning of his prophethood. The first verses revealed commanded him to "Read!" (Iqra). Khadijah (RA) immediately believed and comforted him.

اقْرَأْ بِاسْمِ رَبِّكَ الَّذِي خَلَقَ

"Read in the name of your Lord who created." — Surah Al-Alaq (96:1)

🕌
610-615 CE

Da'wah: Spreading the Message

For three years, the Prophet ﷺ preached secretly, gaining early converts including Abu Bakr (RA), Ali (RA), and Zaid (RA). Then came the command for public preaching. He climbed Mount Safa and proclaimed the message of Tawheed (monotheism), facing severe opposition from the Quraysh.

فَاصْدَعْ بِمَا تُؤْمَرُ وَأَعْرِضْ عَنِ الْمُشْرِكِينَ

"Then declare what you are commanded and turn away from the polytheists." — Surah Al-Hijr (15:94)

😢
615-619 CE

Persecution & Year of Sorrow

The early Muslims faced severe persecution. Bilal (RA) was tortured, and many were killed. Some Muslims migrated to Abyssinia. The Quraysh imposed a boycott on the Muslims for three years. In 619 CE, the "Year of Sorrow," both Khadijah (RA) and Abu Talib passed away.

فَإِنَّ مَعَ الْعُسْرِ يُسْرًا

"For indeed, with hardship will be ease." — Surah Ash-Sharh (94:5)

🌙
619 CE

Al-Isra wal Mi'raj (Night Journey)

In this miraculous night journey, the Prophet ﷺ traveled from Makkah to Jerusalem (Al-Aqsa), then ascended through the heavens, meeting previous prophets and receiving the command for five daily prayers. This journey brought comfort after the Year of Sorrow.

سُبْحَانَ الَّذِي أَسْرَىٰ بِعَبْدِهِ لَيْلًا مِّنَ الْمَسْجِدِ الْحَرَامِ إِلَى الْمَسْجِدِ الْأَقْصَى

"Exalted is He who took His Servant by night from al-Masjid al-Haram to al-Masjid al-Aqsa." — Surah Al-Isra (17:1)

🐪
622 CE

The Hijrah to Madinah

After 13 years in Makkah, Allah commanded the migration to Madinah. The Prophet ﷺ and Abu Bakr (RA) hid in Cave Thawr while Quraysh searched for them. They reached Madinah safely, where the Ansar (helpers) welcomed them joyfully. This marks the start of the Islamic calendar.

إِلَّا تَنصُرُوهُ فَقَدْ نَصَرَهُ اللَّهُ... لَا تَحْزَنْ إِنَّ اللَّهَ مَعَنَا

"Do not grieve; indeed Allah is with us." — Surah At-Tawbah (9:40)

🏛️
622-624 CE

Building the Muslim Community

In Madinah, the Prophet ﷺ established the first mosque (Masjid an-Nabawi), created brotherhood between Muhajirun and Ansar, and drafted the Constitution of Madinah—a groundbreaking document establishing rights and responsibilities for all citizens including Jews and Christians.

وَاعْتَصِمُوا بِحَبْلِ اللَّهِ جَمِيعًا وَلَا تَفَرَّقُوا

"And hold firmly to the rope of Allah all together and do not become divided." — Surah Aal-Imran (3:103)

⚔️
624-627 CE

The Battles: Badr, Uhud, Khandaq

Badr (624 CE): 313 Muslims defeated 1,000 Quraysh—a decisive victory. Uhud (625 CE): A lesson in obedience; Hamza (RA) was martyred. Khandaq/Trench (627 CE): Muslims defended Madinah against a confederation of 10,000 by digging a trench.

وَلَقَدْ نَصَرَكُمُ اللَّهُ بِبَدْرٍ وَأَنتُمْ أَذِلَّةٌ

"And already had Allah given you victory at Badr while you were few in number." — Surah Aal-Imran (3:123)

🤝
628 CE

Treaty of Hudaybiyyah

When Muslims attempted Umrah, Quraysh blocked them. The Prophet ﷺ negotiated a 10-year peace treaty at Hudaybiyyah. Though initially seeming unfavorable, it was called a "clear victory" by Allah, allowing Islam to spread peacefully. Many embraced Islam during this period.

إِنَّا فَتَحْنَا لَكَ فَتْحًا مُّبِينًا

"Indeed, We have given you a clear conquest." — Surah Al-Fath (48:1)

🕋
630 CE

The Conquest of Makkah

After Quraysh broke the treaty, the Prophet ﷺ marched with 10,000 Muslims to Makkah. The city was conquered peacefully. He entered humbly, forgave his former enemies saying "Go, you are free," and purified the Ka'bah of 360 idols, restoring it to monotheistic worship.

وَقُلْ جَاءَ الْحَقُّ وَزَهَقَ الْبَاطِلُ ۚ إِنَّ الْبَاطِلَ كَانَ زَهُوقًا

"Truth has come, and falsehood has departed. Indeed falsehood is bound to depart." — Surah Al-Isra (17:81)

🕌
632 CE

The Farewell Pilgrimage

In his final Hajj, the Prophet ﷺ delivered his famous Farewell Sermon at Mount Arafat to over 100,000 Muslims. He emphasized human equality, rights of women, prohibition of usury, and the importance of following the Quran and Sunnah. It was a comprehensive summary of Islamic teachings.

الْيَوْمَ أَكْمَلْتُ لَكُمْ دِينَكُمْ وَأَتْمَمْتُ عَلَيْكُمْ نِعْمَتِي

"This day I have perfected for you your religion and completed My favor upon you." — Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:3)

🌹
8 June 632 CE

Return to Allah

The Prophet ﷺ passed away on Monday, 12th Rabi al-Awwal, in the arms of his beloved wife Aisha (RA). His final words included prayers for the highest companionship (Ar-Rafiq al-A'la). He was 63 years old. He was buried in Aisha's chamber, now part of Masjid an-Nabawi.

وَمَا مُحَمَّدٌ إِلَّا رَسُولٌ قَدْ خَلَتْ مِن قَبْلِهِ الرُّسُلُ

"Muhammad is not but a messenger. Other messengers have passed before him." — Surah Aal-Imran (3:144)

His Eternal Legacy

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ transformed the world in just 23 years of prophethood. From an orphan in the deserts of Arabia, he became the leader of a growing nation, leaving behind the Quran—unchanged for over 1400 years—and his Sunnah as guidance for all humanity. Today, over 1.8 billion Muslims honor his legacy by following his example of mercy, justice, and devotion to Allah.

اللَّهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَى مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَى آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ

"O Allah, send blessings upon Muhammad and upon the family of Muhammad."

ﷺ Peace and Blessings Be Upon Him ﷺ
